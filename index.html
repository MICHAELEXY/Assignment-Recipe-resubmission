In today's post we are talking about the Bitter leaf soup, known as Ofe Olobu in Igbo, Bitterleaf soup Ofe onugbu (as the Igbos like to call it) is very delicious, this soup is popular because it could be made in more than five different ways by different ethnic groups in Nigeria and it can also be refrigerated for a very long time. This soup can be made with either egusi (melon seeds), ede (cocoa yam), ofor, achi or even ogbono.

Bitter Leaf Soup (Ofe Onugbu)

Recipe Information:

  1. Preparation time: 30 minutes
  2. Cooking time: 1 hour
  3. Number of servings: 4-6
  4. Difficulty level: Medium

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of fresh bitter leaves (well-washed to reduce bitterness)
  • 500g assorted meat (beef, tripe, cow skin/ponmo)
  • 1 medium-sized dry fish or smoked fish
  • 200g cocoyam, boiled and pounded into a paste
  • 1 cup of crayfish, ground
  • 1 tablespoon of ogiri (fermented locust beans)
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • 1 medium-sized onion, chopped
  • 2 hot peppers (atarodo), chopped
  • 1/2 cup of palm oil
  • Salt to taste

Cooking Directions

  1. Prepare the Meat: Wash and season the assorted meat with salt, chopped onions, and one seasoning cube.Place in a pot with a little water and cook until tender.
  2. Add Cocoyam Paste: While the meat is cooking, peel and boil the cocoyam until soft. Pound it in a mortar or use a blender with a little water to form a smooth paste. Set aside
  3. Combine Ingredients: Once the meat is tender, add the washed dry fish, ground crayfish, ogiri, and chopped peppers to the pot. Stir well and allow to cook for about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the Palm Oil: Pour in the palm oil and stir. Let the soup simmer for another 5-7 minutes until the oil is well incorporated.
  5. Thicken the Soup: Gradually add small lumps of the cocoyam paste to the pot. The lumps will dissolve and thicken the soup. If the soup is too thick, you can add a little water to achieve your desired consistency.
  6. Introduce the Bitter Leaves: Add the well-washed bitter leaves to the pot. Stir gently and allow to cook for just a few minutes. Overcooking the bitter leaves can make them tough.
  7. Season and Serve: Taste the soup and add salt and the remaining seasoning cube if necessary. Stir well and turn off the heat.

Tips:

  1. Washing Bitter Leaves: The key to a good bitter leaf soup is properly washed leaves. To remove the bitterness, repeatedly wash the leaves by hand, squeezing and rinsing them in multiple changes of water until the water runs clear and the leaves are no longer bitter. You can also use a little salt to help draw out the bitterness during the washing process.
  2. Cocoyam Substitute:If cocoyam is not available, you can use pounded potato flakes or Achi (a soup thickener) as a substitute.
  3. Protein Variations:Feel free to use other proteins like goat meat, smoked turkey, or fresh fish. The use of a variety of meats adds a complex flavor to the soup.
  4. Flavor Enhancers:Ogiri is a traditional flavor enhancer that gives the soup its unique, authentic taste. If you can't find it, you can omit it, but it will slightly change the flavor profile.
